Somersault

I went to see Somersault on Sunday. GOOD MOVIE, Somersault is a film that stays with you. It has a pace and feel that is similar to reality TV only it doesn’t allow you to notice the concious editing as it goes along. The film is the story of one girls adventure. Heidi seems about 17, she leaves home after her mum finds Heidi snogging her mothers boyfriend. She goes to a holiday town in austrailia where she has some coming of age type stuff. mostly it is about the difference between Love and Sex. I liked this film immensely. It was funny, cool, sad and just connecting on a level which i found has stayed with me. Well worth the admission fee to see.

The Life Aquatic

The Life Aquatic, by Wes Adnerson.What a film. I really enjoyed it. It took a while to warm too, but honestly it was awesome. The guy playing david bowie songs in portugese (I think) was great and genuinely surreal. I don’t really know what to say beyond this but this film was really good. I liked the characters, I liked the set, I liked the pace and tempo of the film. The story line was easy to follow and some genuinely laugh outloud moments. I did wonder if it was an entire mickey take of Documentry makers like Micheal moore. But I think that it is more universal in whom it is seeking to parody and poke fun at. A brilliant way to spent the afternoon of your day off i feel and in the words of Franz Ferdinand “It’s always better in the mattinee”
