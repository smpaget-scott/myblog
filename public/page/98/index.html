carberry people

SO how am I meant to feel. I found out last night that the young people from carberry want to run the program. No help, no input just them. they also want to run the children’s program and the young adults program. old annoyed useless relieved (literal) I don’t know I guess I feel annoyed. I wanted to get the older group together and discuss how it was time for them to raise up and think about what they want from the festival. At the weekend we (staff) discussed what would need to be put in place to support and nurture the knowledge needed to do good youth work. Also to ensure that the older young people and the young people being worked with have a good experience and quality product is produced. why didn’t the young people tell us. frankly we would have been really happy about that and discussed an taken the ideas forward. I really feel rejected. I started doing youth work with these guys 5 years ago this summer. I remember Kimberly asking Dusty why angels are kick ass. Because I had said they were. (I can laugh about it now.) The rejection of any kind of input to the week has to be a negative aspect to the proposal. The text from Kimberly to Grant was very clear about not having help. This is worrying. It makes me wonder what the issues these young people see need to be solved/got round. Perhaps I am one of the issues. Perhaps I am the only one. Perhaps I have just taken it personally because I feel they don’t want/need my help to do the job I currently get paid to do. In the past 3-4 years I have put people in the position of doing carberry without the appropriate support/resourcing/education and seen these people get hurt. I got hurt. I really believe that the “Carberry People” group can do a great job doing the youth work. The question is does this mean the end of carberry for us? I want this to work. the meeting on the 5th of January will be telling.

Barenaked for the Holidays

The BareNaked Ladies gig last night was awesome. It was very good indeed. I think it was really good and started the christmas season off well. I think it was possibly the most exciting gig I have been at. We were down the front on the right hand side. (Ed’s side) I really think that the audience makes a real difference to the gig. Last night there was a real mix of people and a real mix of excitment levels. Barenaked Ladies fans have always been fanatical. (the gig last night sold out and the gig in glasgow on the “everything to everyone” tour was the only British gig to completely sell out. I think a band who can have fun, communicate with the crowd and provide plentyful opportunities to sing along will always be successful. Unfortunate about the Carling Academy being such a lousy venue. No spirt about the place, seats in the venue, air conditioning. I am sure that there are much better venues this band could play. Great night out though. thanks Barenaked Ladies you crazy canadians.
