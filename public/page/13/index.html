Key documents in Scottish Youthwork Policy

I have recently been scouting around the interweb looking for the key documents in the development of the Scottish youth work sector, since youth work was devolved to the Scottish Government (since 1999). They are all there on the interweb, but they are all over the place, having done the searching and found the pdf files, I thought I should catalogue them all here for my own ease of use, and yours if you are interested. If i have missed any please shout in the comments below… 2003 Step it up - University of Strathclyde and The Princes Trust - the first move in defining Scottish youth work and the development of a toolkit for evaluation of the work. Working and learning together to build stronger communities - Scottish Executive Guidance for Community Learning and Development - Scottish Executive - The document which cement the move from Community Education to Community Learning and Development, and with it a change for statuary Youth work and Youth work funding. 2005 Statement on the Nature and Purpose of Youthwork - YouthLink Scotland - YouthLink, the agency for youth work in Scotland provides a key moment, defining of the sectors nature and purpose. (N.B. this link is to the 2009 edition, the text is the same as the 2005 edition but the key documents section has been updated.) 2006 More Choices More Chances: A Strategy to Reduce the Proportion of Young People not in Education, Employment of Training In Scotland - Scottish Executive - The Idea of NEET young people was a perplexing one, youth work was seen as a way to get young people into education, employment or training. 2007 Moving Forward: A Strategy for Improving Young People’s Chances Through Youth Work - Scottish Executive, the first youth work strategy document for Scotland. The key to the strategy was the uncovering the value of youth work and the insertion of youth work to wider policy initiatives. 2008 Professional & National Occupational Standards for Youth Work - Lifelong Learning UK - A framework of what professional youth work standards are. [](Professional & National Occupational Standards for Youth Work - Lifelong Learning UK) 2009 CLD Competencies - The Standards Council for Community Leaning and Development - development of working competencies for Community Learning and Development work. Valuing Young People - Scottish Government - tasks youth work as ‘supporting the outcomes-based approach to performance" under curriculum for excellence and Get it right for every child. Bridging the Gap - YouthLink Scotland, Learning and Teaching Scotland and Scottish Government - implementation of youth work and Schools partnerships under the curriculum for Excellence policy. Subject Benchmark Statement: Youth and Community Work - The Quality Assurance Agency for Higher Education 2010 A Code Of Ethics for Community Learning and Development - Taylor & Sercombe - The Full report into the development of a code of ethics for Community Learning and Development. A Code of Ethics for Community learning and Development, support material - Taylor & Sercombe - Support material for the report above. 2011 A Learning Culture for the Community Learning and Development Sector in Scotland: Continuous Professional Development Strategy - CLD Standards Council for Scotland. 2012 Get It Right For Every Child - Scottish Government 2013 Time to Shine - Scotland Youth Arts Strategy for Ages 0-25 - Creative Scotland - the development of a youth specific national arts strategy. 2014 Our Ambitions for improving the life chances of young people in Scotland, National Youth Work Strategy 2014-2019 - Scottish Government

iOS app Fiete - a game for toddlers

With my daughter getting ready to go to school after the summer. 4 or 5 years of attempting to find good, fun, interesting apps for toddlers is coming to an end. Hence it is time for a short series of app reviews featuring some of our favourite apps for this age group 3-5 year olds. Fiete website- http://www.fiete-app.com/ app store link - https://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/fiete/id586669403?mt=8 Fiete is a simple game, you are accompaning a sailor in a world where thing have went wrong and your job is to help them get better. You help accross 15 scenes (or i would call them senarios) and 3 games, In each scene or senario, you can play and interact with the world making it better. The game runs on iOS maeaning touch is an intergal part of the game, for example onthe scenes opens with this image- you can see a seed, a pot with some soil, and our sailor friend. using your finger you drag the seed into the soil and then get treated to a simple animation of the change you have made. As an app its joy is derived from logical actions from the child, see the problem, find the solution and enjoy the reaction. It is this way of engaging children with game logic that I like. How do you at 14 or 15 become really good at computer games, or logic puzzles, or homework, or perhaps change the world and society? By this really simple encouragement to play, be creative and do the simple things well. How do we create a healthier society? well we give all the sick people the medicine they need to be healthy for free at the point of need. it strikes me that adhering to a simple outworking of this game gives us a bit of hope for the future course of how our society functions, but also this wee game gives us a reminder, that as adults, we are responsable for doing the simple uncomplex changes which makes tomorrow grand. Plus there is a sailor with an hat, seriously, who doesn’t like a sailor in a hat?

reflections on #iasym2014 paper

Last night I gave my paper at the IASYM european conference 2014 in the Czech republic. My reflections are still reasonably fresh. But important. So this post is (primarily for me to reflect on) and secondly anyone else interested. The group was small. I was up against two other papers both of which had very strong appeal, so I had realised in advance I would have a small group. I was conscious that standing at the front talking at people would seem bizarre for the group. So I asked people to move into a small semi-circle and we sat together and talked, the small number lent an informality to the session which I felt was good and helpful. Although I wonder if I talked too fast. As I think standing to speak brings a different physicality and a consciousness that you are addressing people. (Speed of speech was a key point as english was the second language of most people in the room.) The conference had not sent out the papers to everyone, nor was there paper copies available. I asked for some paper copies to be made, and I think it was the right call. It allowed for following along even if you speech was not clear. Again my written english need some serious work. When writing for an international audience, (small number but intelligent and well travelled,) my unique use of english is distracting. Clarity is the key. My paper was 6500words, in order to get into the time for presenting I had to loose 2000 words over the 24hrs before the talk. I need to write more frugally but clearly. The paper gives some models, Sicarts adapted version of gadamers hermeneutical cycle, and Wells four categories of social engagement. They needed a visual element to make them clear, the clarity of the model was lost in my wording. Time wise I think I was on time which I think is always welcome! In the end I think I made a decent go of a first presentation paper. I showed up on time, I my presentation worked, I was able to work computer software in Czech langauge, I read my thing, answered some questions, hopefully looked relaxed, packed everything up and took my rubbish with me. Good Job
