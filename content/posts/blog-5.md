Since Lockdown I have been delivering for the local foodbank. I am not required everyday or week, but I am delivering regularly enough that I know the vans and drivers. It has helped me to see all over the bounds of the Presbytery of Lanark, another body I regularly volunteer with. It has helped me to see parts of the Presbytery I would normally not have seen and consider the links between the Local Church, and local people.
Generosity has always blind. I don't know the people I deliver to and they don't know me. All I know is people have asked for help and the foodbank often gives them more than they are expecting. One person challenged me we were being too generous to someone. I could feel this person desire to sort out the life of the person I was delivering too, but also I rallied against the attack on generosity, seeing the foodbanks response to a request of help, as enabling the behaviours of the recipient. (in my interlocutor's opinion, negative behaviours).
Recently my neighbours have been doing work on their houses. There have been lots of work vans around a number of houses in the area. One neighbour got some work done on the house, but that van looked familiar. A second look identified the van as the food bank delivery van, I then identified the driver, and the familiar containers being brought to the door with food.
When I worked for The Salvation Army a few years back, I was involved in the distribution of Toys and presents to people around the parish. Looking back that glimpse of life give me more reflection to hopefully be a better worker. It was challenging. and fruitful site for discussion and reflection over the time period. But in many ways it was separated from personal life. it was professional life. (Where I lived at the time also had its troubles but there was some division of time and geography between these two events)
This was here. where I am and live. It was someone who isn't a close neighbour or someone I normally socialise with. but someone I see everyday and they're struggling is well hidden from the rest of the neighbours. perhaps the "Everyday" is so fragile because the given, unexamined and assumed nature of it can be punctured by simple actions like someone being generous in response to a hidden cry for help.
Prayer is always good. but prayer on its own, is a pointer to action
> "Your kingdom come, Your will be done, On earth as it is in heaven"
>
> Jesus, in the bible. (Matthew 6v10)
This haunts me and asks me what my part in this is? Where have I contributed to this kingdom coming? Where have I contributed to this will being done? So where do I go? How do I engage with this? How can I give help and assistance when the need for help and assistance is hidden and not asked from myself. How do I maintain the hospitality of a neighbour, when I do not know how to respond well in the situation. I don't know.
