When I left school, I entered into an apprenticeship within engineering construction. I was an apprentice Welder. I didn’t continue that career once I had competed my time and got some codings.
I didn’t appreciate it at the time but welding was providing a long term meditation on joining. The act of taking one thing and joining it to another. The skill it takes to join tricky things. The preparation needed to join two things together.
One of the parts of the process I often thought about was the Non Destructive Testing or NDT. The welds we did on certain artefacts would be loaded in a lorry and sent away for NDT. After a day or two the news come back to us that thing were ok. Only welds that didn’t pass the test was returned to us for destructive re working. Notably the weakest point of the welding process is the area just beside the weld. The join the weld makes is increasingly strong if done properly.
{{< figure src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/img%5F0370.png" alt="" caption="" >}}
The practice of Kintsugi is a Japanese form of joining things, it uses gold where we used different metals, it brings strength and a symbolic meaning that displayed publicly.
I was reminded of this joining practice by a scene in the latest Star Wars DVD the Rise of Skywalker. In the film a character repairs the helmet they destroyed during the last film in the series. The scene of repairing the helmet is very brief but the finished project reminds me of Kintsugi. And that is deliberate. The director of the film was going for t [hat look.](https://www.ign.com/articles/2019/10/01/jj-abrams-addresses-kylo-rens-repaired-mask-in-star-wars-the-rise-of-skywalker)
The idea of joining, welding, reflections upon Kintsugi seem to have something deep. Perhaps this peice sums up some of what I am thinking.
