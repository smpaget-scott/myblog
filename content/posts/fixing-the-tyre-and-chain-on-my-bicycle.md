The bike has been a learning experience for me. At 3 years old the wheel spokes broke. Not too bad but causing a voyage of discovery around what was needed to find a replacement.
**Buying a new wheel rim for my bike.**
The wheel size was 700c which was fine and standard for racing wheels. The only problem was the new wheel set I was going to buy detailed
[![Screen Shot 2015-09-07 at 10.33.30](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/Screen-Shot-2015-09-07-at-10.33.30-300x123.png)](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/Screen-Shot-2015-09-07-at-10.33.30.png)
> Front 100mm, Rear 130mm
Could I find out what this meant. No. I didn't order them although I was tempted various time to do so. So I did a bit more we searching to discover the rear drops of the frame, (The metal bits of the bike the tyre joins onto at the back), have a standard width. Different types of bicycles have different widths, as a hybrid bike my width was 135mm meaning the 130mm tyre would be too small.  There was some discussion if just stuffing a few spacers to pack the wheel in would work or not. I decided not risk it and bought the cheapest wheel I could find in at the car/bicycle/tool warehouse type shop in the industrial estate.
Took it home, fit it, and did my ride the next day. And the day after, also while learning how to change a gearset and a disk brake. #nice. On day three I woke up got ready to discover the rear tyre I had fitted was flat.
**The importance of rim tape**.
[![Screen Shot 2015-09-07 at 10.36.27](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/Screen-Shot-2015-09-07-at-10.36.27-300x253.png)](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/Screen-Shot-2015-09-07-at-10.36.27.png)
So I went off the car/bicycle/tool warehouse type shop in the industrial estate. I got a new inner tube and a new elastic rim tape. Rim tape is essential on 700c tyres as the spokes are put in individually and tightened. Meaning there was lots of drilled holes in the bare rim which needs covering or you get punctures. Initially I had read that existing rim tape can be reused. Trying to get the rim tape off the old wheel was a nightmare. So in a flash of brilliance I tried masking tape. After two rides the tape had failed and the inner tube was punctured. A new rim tape has been fitter and the new inner tube fitted.
One of the problems with this was the new valve caused the inner tube not to sit within the tyre. Meaning the tyre didn't grab the rim and lifter above the rim. By placing the valve (and inner tube) within the tyre I solved that problem well.
**Broken chain.** [![475845398_d4548b0af5_o](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/475845398_d4548b0af5_o-300x198.jpg)](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/475845398_d4548b0af5_o.jpg)
On Friday when out for my run I hit the first decent hill. And changed down a couple of gears, and started to go up the hill. My chain seemed to slip off the gear cog, once, then twice, then a third time until the entire chain snapped. I was 2km from home along an A-road  with no pavements. I walked back and started looking for a new chain.
The guidance for buying a new chain I sit all depends on what gearset you have. 9-speed, 10-speed or 11-speed. I had a 9-speed so a chain was selected and ordered. And it arrived, today. Fitting a new chain is awful. The chain came with a magic link to close the chain. You feed the chain around the bike chain route, and then hold it in place while you make the magic chain and secure it in place which completes the rest of the chain. Apparently there is a tool which aids this process. Alas I did not have one. So I had to try to work it using some long nose pliers. And some manual dexterity in order to make it happen. After a few attempts I managed to get the pieces in the right place. The instructions which came with the chain was ignorable. But it happened and early Monday morning we see how good it goes.
Until Monday!
