1 - Theography
theography is a term coined by Millar is Of Gods and Games, in relation to play theology. He briefly outlines the term as a mapping of where the Holy spirit has been and where it is going to. This mapping activity allows for us to recognise where god is and has been and join in as we find God's activity.
As I listened to some of the papers at the International IASYM conference in Sydney I began to wonder if Geography is a bigger issue for youth ministry than we had given it credit for. If this geography is a bigger issues can mapping some of these theographies be useful in my study?
(I wonder about a question about where young people are christians could reveal alot about why church based youth work is practiced as it currently is. If youth workers assume that young people are only christian at church does that mean that youth work in churches is always going to be focused on youth worship and christian education?)
2 - Provoking well
I had an idea that could be massive. i think it ties in with another idea i have had. but i am wondering how to do this well.
I wonder how far to share the idea before I start work on it?
I wonder how much compromise the idea needs?
I wonder how much time the idea needs?
it is easy to provoke and get a response, but how to provoke well is a trickier task.
