In the spirit of listing thing none has asked me about, I give you some podcasts I listen to.
Player -
There are many podcast players on the market. Open up your device App Store and type in "podcast player" in order to check what is available.
There are three types of podcast player.
1. ones that have special content.
1. ones that insert adverts.
1. ones that do neither.
1. ones that have Exclusive content. Like bbc sounds or Spotify, these players play all available podcasts but have special content which is only available to users of their own app.
1. ones that insert adverts. The dynamics ad insertion is the new hotness to monitise your podcast. There is two problems here. First to make dynamic ads relevant to you the player has to collect information on you. I don't likethis. Second, the ads are taken from the podcast host/producer to a third party, usually the player. (Last year there was a player which forced adverts on podcasts which did not consent to the adverts.)
1. Ones that do neither. Currrently Apple maintains a large detailed database of podcasts which is freely accessible for all players. There podcast feeds are without inserted ads and are not exclusive to Apple.
I use Overcast on iOS for playing podcasts. I choose to pay for it voluntarily, but by default it is free. It uses the iTunes podcast database and it's like its privacy stance.

Some Podcasts I listen to often
- Car Talk from NPR
The NPR radio show Car Talk is funny and often I can guess what is wrong with the cars.
- Fighting Talk from BBC
The BBC five live radio show does not take sports too seriously which is entertaining.
- Friday Night Comedy from BBC
The Friday Night Comedy from BBC Radio 4. Very goood featuring "The News Quiz", "Dead Ringers", "The Now Show" and others.
- Kermode and Mayo's Film Review from BBC
A film show not about films. Informative, knowledgeable, welcoming and good company, and DVD of the week.
- Accidental Tech Podcast
Independent show where Marco Arment, John Siracusa and Casey Liss discuss the latest news from Apple and iOS app development. While they dive deep on topics they are entertaining.
- Conversations with Tyler from the Merceda centre of George Mason University.
Converstations with Tyler allows Tyler Cowen to ask interesting questions of various people. Tyler is an academic economist but his guests are generally not academic economists. For example Reid Hoffman was part of the team that created LinkedIn and PayPal.
