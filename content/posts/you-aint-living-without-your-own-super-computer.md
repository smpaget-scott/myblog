![l-playstation3-logo](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/12/l-playstation3-logo-300x223.jpg)[Astrophysicist Replaces Supercomputer with Eight PlayStation 3s](http://www.wired.com/techbiz/it/news/2007/10/ps3_supercomputer) [Engineer Creates First Academic Playstation 3 Computing Cluster](http://www.physorg.com/news92674403.html)
Using headlines like this, it was brought to the scientific communities attention that [Sony's Playstation 3](http://uk.playstation.com/ps3/) was an open source parallel processing dream. So the idea of clustering a some [Ps3](http://uk.playstation.com/ps3/)'s and making something capable for playing [metal gear solid 4](http://www.konami.jp/mgs4/uk/index.html) and also taking some of work of supercomputers was achieveable and very possible, at a price more affordable than hiring time on a super computer.
One of the academics involved postulated that six [ps3'](http://uk.playstation.com/ps3/) s are equivelant to a 200 node super computer.
[Scientists Write Guide to Build Supercomputer from Sony Playstation 3](http://www.physorg.com/news148749271.html)
Well now they have gone and put the guide on the internet with links to the appropriate linux stuff. so if one night you and your [ps3](http://uk.playstation.com/ps3/) owning friends needs to crunch some big numbers then all the stuff is here for you.
[ps3cluster.org](http://www.ps3cluster.org/index.html)
I love this idea. I don't like it enough to buy a playstation 3 @ £279.99
I wonder if I could persuade the maths department at the local high school to build a super computer for a laugh!
that would be living!
