At teh IASYM online conneference on Thursday there was a brief chat about Training. The context was asking about where is good to engage with PhD level training in the UK. One of the key questions asked was The experience of University PhD's as opposed to Bible COllege PhD's come down to the formational level of the training. The Speak was contrasting Universitys as secular as opposed to Bible Colleges which are confessional.
Having Academic degrees from both Bible Colleges and Universities, I get in general what conflict the speaker was driving at, but I doubt that is my experience. If the speaker is saying the Biblce college has a sarticalur view point they will form you in, I agree. When I went to Bible college, there was a contract of acceptable behaviour and statement f faith you had to agree to in order to be admitted. There was a stop lectured so everyone could communionally meet for worship everyday.
I was on a Youth Work course which was only in college a couple of days and on placement 3 days, as such my reflections are based on that minor involvement. I also would say, (although no one may agree with me that was there at the time.), the Youth work course was profoundly liberal as opposed to the more conservative nature of the rest of the college. I say the rest of the college, I didn't have much experience of the rest of the college, beyond our joint worship times/ joint lectures due to not being there that much. I would also say the Youth work course didn't fit well with the other courses. I was there in the early days. The drop off rate was severe. we started with a class of 30 and graduated 3 years later with 8 of us left. No student on the youth work course had done honours on the course. Philosophically a honours year had been created and documented, but no one had went there yet. The course developed and changed after I left. andI know of one student who returned to a difference part of the college in order to do Masters study. I am grateful fr friendship and how it shaped my thinking in term of community interaction, Praxis, Laissez Faire, and open theism.
When I enrolled in Bible College, I was told they didn't have but were very hopeful of getting secular youth work accreditation. (the body at the time was CEVE). Over the three years I was there, every years they was hopeful of getting this but did not achieve it. The closest we come to being Validated for Secular youth work came in the third year. where the visiting panel interviewed current students. The key conflict seemed to be around Equality and Sexuality.
As there was not an accreditation I then enrolled in the Community Education department of the University of Strathclyde's school of Education. This was a great year of doing a Post Graduate Certificate in Community Education. Alot of thought about discipleship, Power! (a bunch of youth workers in a room it would be strange not to talk about power). It challenged me to become more serious bout theorists, and how I use them. I asked me to become more critical on what I had assumed, and what I was taking for granted from people. The relationship between Youth work, Adult education and Community Development, where they interlinked and were stronger together, than they were apart.
I then enrolled in the University Of Glasgow for a Research Masters. This was great but a bit lonely. Although I audited a class which used Vasquez's material religion as a lens to engage with eh history of religion as a whole.
