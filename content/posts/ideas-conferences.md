... such ideas events prosper because they solve a problem faced by the many at the top of their professions. The much-discussed "death of distance" never happened; globalisation and the profusion of technology makes place more important. Similarly, a world of abundant, instantly accessible information seems to make personal connection more vital. This puts a premium on private events, which force their participants to spend time developing ideas without distraction. ("what's the big idea" by James crabtree in yesterday's financial times.)
