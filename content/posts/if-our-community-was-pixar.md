[![](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/Pixar1.jpg)](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/Pixar1.jpg)
oft of an evening, I read the [Harvard Buisness Review](http://www.hbr.com), this one is from september 2008. Contained within it is a great article from Ed Catmull, Pixar Studio President, called, "How Pixar fosters Creative community." As part of the article, he outlines some of the Pixar communities shared beliefs;
**\- Talent is Rare**
**\- Management's job is not to prevent risk but to build the capacity to to recover when failures occur.**
**\- It must be safe to tell the truth.**
it sounds pretty awesome but i wonder how well each of these would change into some of the communities i live and work in.
but it was the statement directly after these beliefs which caught me.
**\- We must constantly challenge all of our assumptions and search for the flaws that could destroy our culture.**
how, what, hi. \[puzzled but curious face\]
Love it, but could this apply to the communities i am a part of.
i wonder how this could work within a church community?
(sounds as unlike general assembly as anything could be.)
my wanderings are under formed but are wondering.
