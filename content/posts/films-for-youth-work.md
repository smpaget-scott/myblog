On the plane to Australia I had the chance to catch a few films, including the recent film "[Sing street](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt3544112/)". The film centres on a group of young people being young people in Ireland, during the nineteen eighties.
As I watched this I thought this is a great film. Then I wondered if this would be a great film to use in a youth work setting. I think so, and at the end of the film I started a small list of films that say something about being young that I would show a group of 12-15 year olds.
the list currently is.
[Gregory's Girl](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0082477/?ref_=fn_al_tt_1) 1981
[E.T.](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0083866/?ref_=nv_sr_1) 1982
[The Breakfast club](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0088847/?ref_=fn_al_tt_1) 1985
[Clueless](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0112697/?ref_=fn_al_tt_1) 1995
[Romeo & Juliet](http://www.imdb.com/find?ref_=nv_sr_fn&q=romeo+%26+juliet&s=all) 1996
[10 things I hate about you](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0147800/?ref_=nv_sr_2) 1999
[Ping Pong](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0328258/?ref_=nv_sr_4) 2002
[Napoleon Dynamite](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0374900/?ref_=nv_sr_1) 2004
[Pitch Perfect](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1981677/?ref_=nv_sr_1) 2012
[Sing Street](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt3544112/?ref_=fn_al_tt_1) 2016
What should I add or take away?
