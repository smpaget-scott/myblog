[![NadelAufPlatte](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/NadelAufPlatte-300x225.jpg)](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/NadelAufPlatte.jpg) Since christmas I have been listening differently. Thanks to my wife and children I was able to get a new amp and pair it with a vinyl turntable. Yes we are living in the future and I have embrace the technology of 100 years ago!
The amp is a [Marantz PM6004](http://www.whathifi.com/marantz/pm6004/review) and the vinyl is being played on an [Rega RP1](http://www.whathifi.com/rega/rp1/review). They are wired up to a pair of 16 year old [Tannoy Mercury M2.5's](http://www.gramophone.co.uk/editorial/review-tannoy-mercury-m2-loudspeaker?pmtx=orange-all&utm_expid=32540977-3.FNZqseMjTvyRLIewfMgTiA.2&utm_referrer=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.google.co.uk%2Furl%3Fsa%3Dt%26rct%3Dj%26q%3D%26esrc%3Ds%26source%3Dweb%26cd%3D2%26ved%3D0CCkQFjAB%26url%3Dhttp%253A%252F%252Fwww.gramophone.co.uk%252Feditorial%252Freview-tannoy-mercury-m2-loudspeaker%26ei%3DF1bLVJK7DMnuUvOhgsAN%26usg%3DAFQjCNEukn1qoro0S35SJZHqhCanOy4k0g%26bvm%3Dbv.84607526%2Cd.d24%26cad%3Drja).
[![rega](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/rega-300x216.jpg)](/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/rega.jpg)
The sound is warm, solid, clear and fills the room no problem. The next task would be to replace the speakers. The Salvation Army second-hand shop in Perth has a pair of Linn speakers for sale second-hand for £900 which I am sure I could make use of... but i digress.
Music wise I am rocking a mix of vinyl form two sources.
Source one is various things I have collected from way back, from when I would DJ for people and briefly volunteered in a second-hand shop. [Frampton comes alive](http://www.discogs.com/Peter-Frampton-Frampton-Comes-Alive/release/401512), [Dylan at the budokan](http://www.discogs.com/Bob-Dylan-Bob-Dylan-At-Budokan/release/373350), [Dire Straits](http://www.discogs.com/Dire-Straits-Dire-Straits/release/1308001), and others.
Source two is more recent vinyl which has focused around albums from the recent best of lists i have made; [the national high violet](http://www.discogs.com/National-High-Violet/release/2267150), [my morning jacket circutal](http://www.discogs.com/My-Morning-Jacket-Circuital/release/2924068), and other records I think could benefit from being heard on vinyl; [over the Rhine meet me at the edge of the world](http://www.discogs.com/Over-the-Rhine-Meet-Me-at-the-Edge-of-the-World/release/5542046), [the innocence mission we walked in song](http://www.discogs.com/Innocence-Mission-We-Walked-In-Song/release/2545877) and [the fanfarlo](http://www.discogs.com/search/?q=fanfarlo&format_exact=Vinyl&format_exact=Album) albums.
Vinyl is usually more expensive than cd or download, but it does have some advantages. Firstly vinyl has a physicality to it which is significant. The 180gram vinyl has some weight to it in comparison to CD's. Playing music physically takes some commitment and attention. The turning of a record takes care where as CDs can be thrown around reasonably easily. (Throw about an iPod at your own risk!).
I am not sure if the music is better or not but I feel a lot more involved and conscious of the process which is surely a good thing.
This corresponds with an increased consideration vinyl within the mainstream media. [for example John Harris is great in this article on the guardian long reads website on the dangers of the vinyl revival.](http://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/jan/07/-sp-vinyls-difficult-comeback) (The accompanying podcast is also worth subscribing to). and this article [from the New York Times describing some deeper issues around vinyls comeback](http://www.nytimes.com/2014/10/19/magazine/streaming-music-has-left-me-adrift.html?_r=1). Plus Tom Gauld's illustration is fabulous for it.
so there it is.
The chances are I will be writing or tweeting about vinyl a bit more, and it will be noticeable. Getting excited about [Steve Taylor and the Perfect Foils choose your own colour of Vinyl release of Goliath](http://www.soundsfamilyre.com/artists/steve-taylor-the-perfect-foil/steve-taylor-the-perfect-foil-discography/goliath/), or stumbling across the 180 gram coloured vinyl reissue of S [ufjan Stevens Seven Swans](http://www.discogs.com/Sufjan-Stevens-Seven-Swans/release/1284052). It's Just a part of me which is ok, and geeky, and abiding.
